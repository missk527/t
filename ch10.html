Metals and Non-metals Questions

  1. Where are most metals found on the periodic table?
    → Towards the bottom left.
  2. Are most elements metals or non-metals?
    → Metals.
  3. What kind of ions do metals form?
    → Positive ions.
  4. Give one example of a positive ion formed by a metal.
    → Calcium ion (Ca²⁺).
  5. What kind of ions do non-metals usually form?
    → Negative ions.
  6. Give one example of a negative ion.
    → Chloride ion (Cl⁻).
  7. Why do atoms react?
    → To achieve a full outer electron shell.
  8. What do atoms want in their outer shell?
    → A full set of electrons.
  9. What kind of elements are found on the left side of the periodic table?
    → Metals with few outer electrons.
  10. How many outer electrons does calcium have?
    → Two.
  11. Why can calcium easily form a positive ion?
    → It only needs to lose two electrons.
  12. How many electrons does chlorine have in its outer shell?
    → Seven.
  13. Why can’t chlorine easily form a positive ion?
    → It would need to lose seven electrons, which requires too much energy.
  14. Which part of the periodic table has the most reactive metals?
    → The bottom of a group.
  15. Why are metals more reactive down a group?
    → Their outer electrons are farther from the nucleus and easier to lose.
  16. How many electron shells does lead (Pb) have?
    → Six.
  17. Why does the nucleus hold outer electrons less strongly in heavy metals?
    → The outer electrons are far from the positive nucleus.
  18. What happens when an atom loses electrons?
    → It forms a positive ion.
  19. Why do metals further down the group lose electrons more easily?
    → The nucleus has a weaker hold on distant outer electrons.
  20. What type of bonding do all metals have?
    → Metallic bonding.
  21. Are metallic bonds strong or weak?
    → Strong.
  22. What does “malleable” mean?
    → Can be bent or hammered into shape without breaking.
  23. Why can metals be used in wires?
    → They conduct electricity well.
  24. Do metals have high or low melting points?
    → High.
  25. What does “sonorous” mean?
    → Makes a ringing sound when struck.
  26. Give two properties of metals.
    → Strong and shiny (also conductive, malleable).
  27. What is the appearance of non-metals?
    → Dull in color.
  28. Are non-metals brittle or flexible?
    → Brittle.
  29. Do non-metals have high or low melting points?
    → Low.
  30. Are many non-metals solid, liquid, or gas at room temperature?
    → Gas.
  31. Are non-metals good or poor conductors of electricity?
    → Poor conductors.
  32. Do non-metals have high or low density?
    → Low density.
  33. Where are transition metals found on the periodic table?
    → In the center.
  34. What are transition metals considered to be?
    → Typical metals.
  35. Can transition metals form more than one ion?
    → Yes, they can.
  36. Give one example of a transition metal that forms several ions.
    → Chromium (Cr²⁺, Cr³⁺, Cr⁶⁺).
  37. What colors can transition metal ions be?
    → Blue, green, or orange.
  38. What do catalysts do in chemical reactions?
    → Speed up the reaction.
  39. Is a catalyst used up during a reaction?
    → No, it is not used up.
  40. Name one transition metal used as a catalyst and the process it’s used in.
    → Iron in the Haber process (to make ammonia).
