Plant Organization and Leaf Structure Questions

  1. What levels of organization do plants have?
    → Cells, tissues, organs, and organ systems.
  2. What do similar plant cells combine to form?
    → Tissues.
  3. What do different types of tissues combine to form?
    → Organs.
  4. What do multiple organs combine to form?
    → Organ systems.
  5. What is the leaf classified as?
    → An organ.
  6. Which plant parts form the transport organ system?
    → The leaf, stem, and roots.
  7. What is the main function of the leaf system?
    → To transport substances around the plant.
  8. What is shown when we look at a leaf cross-section?
    → The internal structure of the leaf.
  9. What is the main function of leaves?
    → Photosynthesis.
  10. What two materials are needed for photosynthesis?
    → Carbon dioxide and water.
  11. Where does the water for photosynthesis come from?
    → From the soil.
  12. Through which tissue is water transported upward?
    → Xylem.
  13. How does carbon dioxide enter the leaf?
    → By diffusion from the outside air.
  14. What are the tiny holes on a leaf surface called?
    → Stomata.
  15. Where are most stomata located?
    → On the lower epidermis.
  16. What type of tissue forms the lower epidermis?
    → Epidermal tissue.
  17. After entering through stomata, where does carbon dioxide move next?
    → Into the spongy mesophyll tissue.
  18. Why does the spongy mesophyll have air spaces?
    → To allow gases to diffuse easily through the leaf.
  19. What is the next layer after the spongy mesophyll?
    → The palisade mesophyll layer.
  20. Which leaf layer contains many chloroplasts?
    → The palisade mesophyll.
  21. What process happens mainly in the palisade layer?
    → Photosynthesis.
  22. Why are upper epidermis cells nearly transparent?
    → To let sunlight pass through to the palisade cells below.
  23. Which two tissues form the leaf’s transport tubes?
    → Xylem and phloem.
  24. What does the phloem carry away from the leaf?
    → Sugars made by photosynthesis.
  25. What does the xylem bring to the leaf?
    → Water from the roots.
  26. What problem do leaves face on both surfaces?
    → Water loss.
  27. What is the purpose of the waxy cuticle?
    → To reduce water loss from the leaf.
  28. What is the waxy cuticle made of?
    → Lipids (a thin waterproof layer).
  29. Why can water easily diffuse out from stomata?
    → Because they are open gaps to the air.
  30. Why must leaves keep stomata open for a short time only?
    → To absorb enough carbon dioxide but minimize water loss.
  31. What are the cells that control the opening of each stoma called?
    → Guard cells.
  32. What happens to guard cells when the plant has plenty of water?
    → They become turgid and open the stomata.
  33. What term describes hydrated guard cells?
    → Turgid.
  34. What happens to guard cells when the plant loses water?
    → They become flaccid and close the stomata.
  35. What term describes water-deficient guard cells?
    → Flaccid.
  36. Why do guard cells close stomata at night?
    → Because photosynthesis stops and carbon dioxide isn’t needed.
  37. Why are most stomata found on the underside of the leaf?
    → It’s cooler and shaded, so less water evaporates.
  38. What tissue acts like plant stem cells?
    → Meristem tissue.
  39. Where are meristem tissues found?
    → At the growing tips of roots and shoots.
  40. What can meristem cells do as the plant grows?
    → Differentiate into many types of plant cells.
