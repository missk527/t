Answer Key – Word & Symbol Equations (40 questions)

  1. A word equation.
  2. Methane (CH₄) and oxygen (O₂).
  3. Carbon dioxide (CO₂) and water (H₂O).
  4. A single arrow (→).
  5. That the reactants fully react to form the products.
  6. Reactants.
  7. Products.
  8. Word equations use names; symbol equations use chemical formulas.
  9. CH₄ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O (unbalanced form).
  10. Because oxygen is diatomic; it exists as O₂.

Molecules that Exist as Pairs

  1. As O₂ (diatomic oxygen).
  2. Chlorine (Cl₂) and nitrogen (N₂).
  3. Because chlorine is diatomic; single Cl would change the substance.
  4. N₂.
  5. O₃ is ozone, a different substance—not the oxygen used in combustion.

Balancing Chemical Equations

  1. To conserve atoms/mass on both sides (law of conservation of mass).
  2. 1 carbon.
  3. 4 hydrogens.
  4. 2 oxygens.
  5. 1 carbon.
  6. 2 hydrogens.
  7. 3 oxygens (2 in CO₂ + 1 in H₂O).
  8. Changing subscripts changes the substance.
  9. The number of molecules/moles (stoichiometric coefficients).
  10. By convention coefficients are whole numbers; fractions change counting.

Balancing the Methane Example

  1. 2 (in front of O₂).
  2. 4 oxygen atoms on the left.
  3. Put 2 in front of H₂O.
  4. CH₄ + 2 O₂ → CO₂ + 2 H₂O.
  5. 2 water molecules.

Second Example (Sulfuric Acid + Sodium Hydroxide)

  1. H₂SO₄ (sulfuric acid) and NaOH (sodium hydroxide).
  2. Na₂SO₄ (sodium sulfate) and H₂O (water).
  3. 1 sodium.
  4. 2 sodiums.
  5. Sodium (sulfur is already balanced).
  6. 2 (in front of NaOH).
  7. Hydrogen and oxygen on the products side still need adjustment.
  8. Add one more water molecule to the right.
  9. 2 water molecules.
  10. H₂SO₄ + 2 NaOH → Na₂SO₄ + 2 H₂O.
