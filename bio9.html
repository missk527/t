Exchange and Surface Area to Volume Ratio Questions

  1. What must an organism do to survive in its environment?
    → Exchange substances with its environment.
  2. What are useful substances taken in by organisms called?
    → Nutrients or useful substrates.
  3. What do organisms excrete when they remove materials they don’t need?
    → Waste products.
  4. What term describes keeping a stable internal environment?
    → Homeostasis.
  5. How do single-celled organisms exchange substances with their environment?
    → Directly through their cell surface or cell wall.
  6. Why can single-celled organisms exchange substances easily?
    → They are very small, so substances diffuse quickly across the surface.
  7. What feature of small organisms allows fast diffusion?
    → A large surface area to volume ratio.
  8. What does “surface area to volume ratio” mean?
    → The amount of surface area available for exchange compared to the organism’s volume.
  9. What happens to the surface area to volume ratio as an organism gets larger?
    → It decreases.
  10. What effect does a smaller surface area to volume ratio have on diffusion rate?
    → Diffusion becomes slower and less efficient.
  11. Why can’t large organisms rely only on diffusion?
    → It would take too long to transport substances to all cells.
  12. Give one example of a single-celled organism mentioned in the speech.
    → Bacteria.
  13. In bacteria, which gas diffuses into the cell during respiration?
    → Oxygen.
  14. In bacteria, which gas diffuses out as waste during respiration?
    → Carbon dioxide.
  15. What is the surface area of a cube with sides of 1 cm?
    → 6 cm².
  16. What is the volume of a cube with sides of 1 cm?
    → 1 cm³.
  17. What is the surface area of a cube with sides of 10 cm?
    → 600 cm².
  18. What is the volume of a cube with sides of 10 cm?
    → 1000 cm³.
  19. What is the surface area to volume ratio of the 1 cm cube (the “mouse”)?
    → 6 : 1.
  20. What is the surface area to volume ratio of the 10 cm cube (the “elephant”)?
    → 0.6 : 1.
  21. What does the cube example show about size and surface area to volume ratio?
    → As organisms get bigger, their SA : V ratio becomes smaller.
  22. Why do larger organisms need specialized exchange systems?
    → Because diffusion alone is too slow to meet their needs.
  23. Name one example of a specialized exchange surface in animals.
    → Alveoli in the lungs.
  24. Name one example of a specialized exchange surface in plants.
    → Leaves or root hair cells.
  25. What are the small structures in the lungs that exchange gases called?
    → Alveoli.
  26. What are the structures in the small intestine that absorb nutrients called?
    → Villi.
  27. What plant structure increases water and mineral absorption?
    → Root hair cells.
  28. What structure allows gas exchange in fish?
    → Gills.
  29. What is the first general feature of an effective exchange surface?
    → A large surface area relative to its volume.
  30. How does a large surface area help diffusion?
    → It allows more particles to pass through at once.
  31. What is the second feature of an effective exchange surface?
    → Very thin membranes.
  32. Why are thin membranes important for diffusion?
    → They shorten the diffusion distance, speeding up exchange.
  33. What is the third feature of an effective exchange surface?
    → Permeable membranes.
  34. Why must exchange membranes be permeable?
    → So substances can diffuse through them easily.
  35. What is the fourth feature of an effective exchange surface?
    → A maintained diffusion or concentration gradient.
  36. What provides the diffusion gradient in the lungs?
    → A constant supply of oxygen from air entering the lungs.
  37. How does the bloodstream help maintain a diffusion gradient?
    → It continually removes oxygen and brings in carbon dioxide.
  38. What is the role of oxygen in gas exchange?
    → It diffuses into the blood for respiration.
  39. What waste gas is removed during respiration?
    → Carbon dioxide.
  40. Summarize one reason why diffusion alone is not enough for large organisms.
    → Because their small SA : V ratio makes diffusion too slow to supply all cells efficiently.
