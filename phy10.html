Wind & Solar Power — Questions

40 quick Q&A for revision

  1. What two renewable energy sources are discussed in the video?→ Wind and solar power.
  2. What device is used to capture wind power?→ Wind turbines.
  3. Where are wind turbines usually placed?→ In exposed areas like moors or coasts.
  4. Why are moors and coasts good locations for wind turbines?→ Because they have strong, consistent winds.
  5. What does each wind turbine contain inside?→ A generator.
  6. What turns the generator inside a wind turbine?→ The spinning blades.
  7. What kind of energy is converted by the spinning blades?→ Kinetic energy.
  8. What type of energy does the generator produce?→ Electrical energy.
  9. Where is the generated electricity transported to?→ The national grid.
  10. What captures solar power?→ Solar cells (solar panels).
  11. What form do solar cells usually take?→ Panels.
  12. What do solar panels generate directly from sunlight?→ Electric current.
  13. What natural process is solar energy compared to?→ Photosynthesis.
  14. Name a small device that uses solar energy well.→ A watch or calculator.
  15. Why don’t solar-powered watches and calculators need new batteries?→ Because sunlight powers them directly.
  16. Where are solar panels especially useful besides small devices?→ In remote regions.
  17. Why are solar panels useful in the Australian outback?→ Because it’s remote and not connected to the power grid.
  18. How is solar energy being used on a larger scale today?→ To generate electricity for entire towns.
  19. What is a shared advantage of wind and solar power?→ Very low running costs.
  20. What greenhouse gas do they not produce?→ Carbon dioxide (CO₂).
  21. Do wind and solar have high or low running costs?→ Low.
  22. Do wind and solar produce pollutants while operating?→ No.
  23. What is a shared disadvantage of wind and solar?→ High upfront (installation) costs.
  24. What makes solar cell manufacturing a bit polluting?→ The production process releases pollutants.
  25. Why do wind and solar depend on weather conditions?→ They need wind or sunlight to generate electricity.
  26. What happens if the wind doesn’t blow?→ Wind turbines don’t produce power.
  27. What happens if the sun doesn’t shine?→ Solar panels don’t produce power.
  28. Can wind or solar increase supply instantly during peak demand?→ No, they can’t.
  29. Why do they take up a lot of space?→ Many units are needed to generate large amounts of power.
  30. What complaint do some people have about wind turbines’ appearance?→ They don’t look nice.
  31. What other complaint do people make about wind turbines?→ They are too noisy.
  32. Do wind and solar damage the environment during use?→ No.
  33. Are wind and solar becoming cheaper or more expensive each year?→ Cheaper.
  34. What type of energy do wind turbines convert into electricity?→ Kinetic energy.
  35. What type of energy do solar cells convert into electricity?→ Light (solar) energy.
  36. Which has higher upfront costs — fossil fuels or renewables?→ Renewables (wind and solar).
  37. Why are high upfront costs a disadvantage?→ Because they require large investment before generating profit.
  38. Are wind and solar good long-term energy options?→ Yes, they’re sustainable and clean.
  39. Do wind and solar emit carbon dioxide while running?→ No.
  40. What is the overall trend for renewable energy technology costs?→ They are decreasing every year.
