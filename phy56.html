Answers – Newton’s First & Second Laws, Circular Motion, and Inertia

  1. Newton’s First Law says that an object’s motion will not change unless a resultant force acts on it.
  2. A resultant (net) force is required to change the motion of an object.
  3. If the resultant force is zero, the object’s motion stays the same — no change in speed or direction.
  4. A stationary object with zero resultant force will remain stationary.
  5. A moving object with zero resultant force will continue moving at constant velocity.
  6. Newton’s Second Law says a non-zero resultant force causes acceleration.
  7. No, acceleration can mean speeding up, slowing down, starting, stopping, or changing direction.
  8. It would speed up.
  9. It would slow down or stop, because the force acts opposite the motion.
  10. Yes, acceleration can change only direction while keeping the same speed.
  11. Because velocity depends on both speed and direction, so changing direction means changing velocity.
  12. Velocity is determined by speed and direction.
  13. Yes, the Moon is accelerating.
  14. Because its direction constantly changes as it orbits Earth.
  15. The gravitational force of Earth keeps the Moon in orbit.
  16. The inward force acts perpendicular to motion, toward the center (centripetal).
  17. The mass of the particle was 0.25 kg.
  18. The forces were 30 N to the left and 42 N to the right.
  19. The resultant force was 12 N to the right (42 − 30).
  20. The equation is F = m a.
  21. Rearranged: a = F / m.
  22. The acceleration was 48 m/s² (12 ÷ 0.25).
  23. If the resultant force doubles, acceleration also doubles.
  24. If mass increases, acceleration decreases (for the same force).
  25. Inertia is the tendency of an object to resist changes in its motion.
  26. An object at rest will stay at rest.
  27. An object in motion will stay in motion at constant velocity.
  28. Inertial mass measures how difficult it is to change an object’s velocity.
  29. Inertial mass = force ÷ acceleration (m = F / a).
  30. A large mass needs more force to change its velocity.
  31. For a given force, a large mass has small acceleration.
  32. If no resultant force acts, velocity does not change.
  33. No — it might slow down, stop, or change direction, not only speed up.
  34. A force that is not zero is called a non-zero resultant force.
  35. The symbol for force is F.
  36. The symbol for mass is m.
  37. The symbol for acceleration is a.
  38. Force is measured in newtons (N).
  39. Acceleration is measured in metres per second squared (m/s²).
  40. In circular motion, speed is constant but velocity changes because the direction is constantly changing.
