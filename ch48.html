Reversible Reactions & Equilibrium — Questions

40 quick Q&A for revision

  1. What symbol shows a one-way reaction?→ A single arrow (→).
  2. What does the right-pointing arrow (→) mean in a chemical equation?→ Reactants change into products only (one direction).
  3. What does a double arrow (⇌) represent?→ A reversible reaction.
  4. What type of reaction can go both forward and backward?→ A reversible reaction.
  5. What is the forward reaction in the example of ammonium chloride?→ NH₄Cl → NH₃ + HCl.
  6. What is the backward reaction in that same example?→ NH₃ + HCl → NH₄Cl.
  7. Why is the ammonium chloride reaction called reversible?→ Because both forward and backward reactions can occur.
  8. What happens to the backward reaction rate at the beginning?→ It is zero (no products yet).
  9. Why is the forward reaction fast at the start?→ Because there is lots of reactant and no product.
  10. What happens to the forward reaction rate over time?→ It slows down.
  11. What happens to the backward reaction rate over time?→ It speeds up.
  12. When both reaction rates become equal, what do we say the system has reached?→ Equilibrium.
  13. At equilibrium, do reactions stop completely?→ No.
  14. What continues to happen at equilibrium?→ Both forward and backward reactions continue at equal rates.
  15. At equilibrium, do concentrations of reactants and products keep changing?→ No, they remain constant.
  16. What do we call it when the concentrations stay constant but reactions still occur?→ Dynamic equilibrium.
  17. Are the concentrations of reactants and products always the same at equilibrium?→ No, they can differ.
  18. What is meant by the “position of equilibrium”?→ It shows whether there are more products or more reactants.
  19. When there are more products, which side does the equilibrium lie on?→ To the right.
  20. When there are more reactants, which side does the equilibrium lie on?→ To the left.
  21. Can the position of equilibrium change?→ Yes.
  22. What factor can move the position of equilibrium to the right?→ Heating (for endothermic forward reactions).
  23. What factor can move it to the left?→ Cooling.
  24. When we heat the ammonium chloride reaction, what happens?→ It shifts to the right (more NH₃ and HCl).
  25. When we cool the reaction, which direction is favored?→ The left (more NH₄Cl forms).
  26. What kind of system is needed for equilibrium to be reached?→ A closed system.
  27. What does a closed system mean?→ Nothing can enter or leave — reactants and products stay inside.
  28. Why can’t equilibrium be reached in an open system?→ Because escaping products prevent balance.
  29. What are the two types of energy changes involved in reversible reactions?→ Endothermic and exothermic.
  30. Is one direction of a reversible reaction endothermic or exothermic?→ One is endothermic.
  31. Which direction is the reverse reaction in terms of heat flow?→ It is exothermic.
  32. What does “endothermic” mean?→ Absorbs heat from surroundings.
  33. What does “exothermic” mean?→ Releases heat to surroundings.
  34. What color is hydrated copper sulfate?→ Blue.
  35. What color is anhydrous copper sulfate?→ White.
  36. What happens when hydrated copper sulfate is heated?→ It loses water and turns white (anhydrous form).
  37. What happens when water is added back to the anhydrous copper sulfate?→ It turns blue again (hydrated form).
  38. Which direction (forward or backward) is exothermic in the copper sulfate example?→ The backward (hydration) reaction.
  39. At equilibrium, what must be true about the forward and backward rates?→ They are equal.
  40. What happens to the position of equilibrium when temperature conditions change?→ It shifts left or right depending on whether heating or cooling occurs.
