Tumors and Cancer — 40 Short Questions with Answers

  1. What is a common misconception about tumors and cancer?
    → That the two terms mean the same thing.
  2. What is a tumor?
    → An abnormal mass of cells.
  3. What causes a tumor to form?
    → Uncontrolled growth and division of cells.
  4. What are the two main types of tumors?
    → Benign and malignant.
  5. Which type of tumor is considered cancer?
    → Malignant tumors.
  6. What happens to cells in a tumor?
    → They grow and divide uncontrollably.
  7. How is a benign tumor different from a malignant tumor?
    → Benign tumors stay in one place; malignant tumors spread.
  8. Where are benign tumors usually contained?
    → Within one area, usually inside a membrane.
  9. Why are benign tumors not normally dangerous?
    → Because they don’t invade or spread to other tissues.
  10. Do benign tumors spread to other parts of the body?
    → No, they remain localized.
  11. What term is used for tumors that invade other tissues?
    → Malignant tumors.
  12. Why are malignant tumors more dangerous?
    → They can invade tissues and spread throughout the body.
  13. What can malignant tumors form when they spread?
    → Secondary tumors.
  14. What are “secondary tumors”?
    → New tumors that form in different parts of the body.
  15. Why are malignant tumors classified as cancer?
    → Because they spread and cause serious damage to tissues.
  16. What kind of damage can malignant tumors cause to the body?
    → They destroy tissues and interfere with organ function.
  17. Give one lifestyle risk factor that increases the chance of developing cancer.
    → Smoking.
  18. What is one strong link you probably already know about?
    → The link between smoking and lung cancer.
  19. Which cancer is most strongly linked to smoking?
    → Lung cancer.
  20. Name three other cancers linked to smoking.
    → Mouth, stomach, and cervical cancers.
  21. What lifestyle factor besides smoking increases cancer risk?
    → Obesity.
  22. Which cancers are linked to obesity?
    → Bowel, liver, and kidney cancers.
  23. What kind of light exposure is linked to skin cancer?
    → Ultraviolet (UV) light.
  24. Where do humans get ultraviolet light exposure from?
    → The sun and sunbeds.
  25. How does UV radiation cause skin cancer?
    → It damages skin cells.
  26. Which lifestyle habit is linked to an increased risk of liver cancer?
    → Drinking alcohol.
  27. Are all risk factors for cancer lifestyle choices?
    → No, some are genetic.
  28. What kind of risk factors can we inherit?
    → Genetic risk factors.
  29. What do we inherit from our parents that can make us more likely to develop cancer?
    → Certain genes.
  30. What are BRCA genes linked to?
    → Increased cancer susceptibility.
  31. Which two types of cancer are associated with BRCA genes?
    → Breast and ovarian cancers.
  32. Why might obesity increase the risk of certain cancers?
    → It affects hormone and cell growth regulation.
  33. What organ is most affected by alcohol-related cancer?
    → The liver.
  34. What does it mean if cells undergo “uncontrolled growth and division”?
    → They multiply without normal regulation, forming tumors.
  35. Why is it important to distinguish between benign and malignant tumors?
    → Because only malignant tumors are cancerous and life-threatening.
  36. What is the membrane around benign tumors responsible for?
    → Containing the tumor in one place.
  37. What can happen when malignant cells spread to new parts of the body?
    → They form secondary tumors.
  38. Why are malignant tumors potentially fatal?
    → They disrupt vital body functions and organs.
  39. Give two examples of environmental or lifestyle cancer risk factors.
    → Smoking and UV light exposure.
  40. Give one example of a genetic cancer risk factor.
    → Inherited BRCA gene mutations.
