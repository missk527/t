Fossils, Evolution & Extinction — Short Questions with Answers

  1. What are fossils?
    → Remains or traces left behind by long-dead organisms.
  2. Give two examples of what a fossil might be.
    → A skeleton turned to rock; an impression like a footprint.
  3. Why are fossils important to scientists?
    → They show how life forms have changed over time.
  4. What does the study of fossils tell us about organisms over time?
    → It shows gradual evolutionary changes.
  5. Fossils provide evidence for which major scientific theory?
    → Evolution.
  6. How many main ways of fossil formation are described in the video?
    → Three.
  7. What is the most common way fossils form?
    → Gradual replacement by minerals.
  8. In gradual replacement by minerals, which body parts decay slowly?
    → Bones, teeth, and shells.
  9. What replaces the decaying hard parts to form fossils?
    → Minerals.
  10. What is left behind after mineral replacement is complete?
    → A rock-like replica of the original structure.
  11. What do we call the type of fossil that forms when an organism buried in clay decays, leaving a mould?
    → A cast.
  12. Give one example of something that can leave a cast.
    → An animal burrow or plant root.
  13. What is an impression fossil?
    → A mark or imprint left by an organism.
  14. Give one example of an impression fossil.
    → A footprint.
  15. Name one place where complete preservation can occur with no decay.
    → Amber, tar pits, glaciers, or peat bogs.
  16. Why does decay not happen in amber or tar pits?
    → Because there is no oxygen or moisture.
  17. Why can organisms be preserved in glaciers?
    → It’s too cold for microorganisms to survive.
  18. Why do peat bogs prevent decay?
    → They are too acidic for microorganisms.
  19. The oldest fossils found so far are roughly how old?
    → Around 3 to 4 billion years old.
  20. Why is the fossil record incomplete?
    → Many fossils are missing or destroyed; early life forms decayed quickly.
  21. What kind of organisms were the earliest life forms?
    → Soft-bodied organisms.
  22. Why are fossils of early soft-bodied organisms unlikely to exist?
    → They decayed too quickly to fossilize.
  23. Name one geological process that can destroy fossils over time.
    → Volcanoes or earthquakes.
  24. How can the movement of tectonic plates affect fossils?
    → It can destroy or distort them through heat and pressure.
  25. What is extinction?
    → When no individuals of a species remain.
  26. What does it mean when a species has gone extinct?
    → The species has completely died out.
  27. Give one possible cause of extinction related to environmental change.
    → Habitat destruction or rapid global warming.
  28. Give one example of a human activity that has caused extinction.
    → Over-hunting.
  29. How can a new predator cause a species to go extinct?
    → By killing them faster than they can reproduce.
  30. How can a new disease lead to extinction?
    → It spreads through a population and kills them.
  31. What does it mean when a new species “out-competes” another?
    → It uses food or resources more effectively, leaving none for the other species.
  32. What catastrophic event is thought to have wiped out the dinosaurs?
    → An asteroid impact.
  33. Approximately how many years ago did the dinosaur extinction event occur?
    → About 66 million years ago.
  34. What country did the asteroid that wiped out the dinosaurs hit?
    → Mexico.
  35. What evidence from fossils shows that evolution has occurred?
    → Fossils reveal small, gradual changes between species over time.
  36. Why is it difficult to know how life first began on Earth?
    → Early fossils are missing, and soft-bodied organisms decayed too fast.
  37. What conditions stop microorganisms from carrying out decay?
    → Lack of oxygen, lack of moisture, low temperature, or high acidity.
  38. Name two natural materials that can trap and preserve organisms.
    → Amber and tar.
  39. Which part of an organism is least likely to form a fossil?
    → Soft tissue.
  40. Summarize one reason why studying fossils is important for understanding life on Earth.
    → They help us understand how species evolved and why some became extinct.
