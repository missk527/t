Energy Resources — Questions & Answers

  1. What was humanity’s main source of energy for most of history?
    → Wood.
  2. What were two main uses of wood as an energy source?
    → Heating and cooking.
  3. Around how many years ago did energy use start changing dramatically?
    → About 200 years ago.
  4. What do many people in developing countries still rely on for energy?
    → Wood.
  5. Name three examples of non-renewable energy resources.
    → Coal, oil, and natural gas.
  6. What term is used to describe coal, oil, and natural gas collectively?
    → Fossil fuels.
  7. What is another type of non-renewable energy mentioned besides fossil fuels?
    → Nuclear energy.
  8. In the past 50 years, which type of energy resources have risen rapidly in use?
    → Renewable resources.
  9. Name three renewable energy resources mentioned in the speech.
    → Solar, wind, and hydroelectric.
  10. What are biofuels made from?
    → Plants or other organic matter (biomass).
  11. What is tidal energy generated by?
    → The movement of tides (sea water).
  12. In the UK, most energy is used for which two sectors?
    → Transport and domestic uses.
  13. What does “transport” refer to in this context?
    → Vehicles such as cars, trains, planes, and ships.
  14. Give two examples of transport vehicles listed in the speech.
    → Cars and trains.
  15. What two fuels are mainly used for road vehicles?
    → Petrol and diesel.
  16. Which fuel is used for airplanes?
    → Kerosene.
  17. What type of fuel is kerosene made from?
    → Oil (a fossil fuel).
  18. What were trains traditionally powered by?
    → Coal.
  19. What major historical event was driven by coal-powered trains?
    → The Industrial Revolution.
  20. What powers most trains today?
    → Electricity.
  21. What renewable transport fuel is made from plants?
    → Biofuels.
  22. What trend has been increasing in road transport recently?
    → The use of biofuels and electric cars.
  23. What does “domestic uses” mean?
    → Energy used in households.
  24. Give two examples of domestic uses of energy.
    → Cooking and heating.
  25. What is the UK’s main energy source for heating?
    → Natural gas.
  26. What is natural gas burned to heat?
    → Water.
  27. How is thermal energy from hot water transferred to rooms?
    → Through radiators.
  28. Name another non-renewable fuel used in fireplaces for heat.
    → Coal.
  29. What do solar water heaters use to heat water?
    → The Sun’s energy.
  30. How can biofuels be used for heating?
    → They can be burned like coal.
  31. What determines whether electricity is renewable or non-renewable?
    → How it is generated.
  32. Is electricity itself considered renewable or non-renewable?
    → Neither; it depends on the energy source.
  33. What does the UK electricity pie chart show as the main source?
    → Fossil fuels.
  34. Roughly what percentage of UK electricity comes from fossil fuels?
    → About 60%.
  35. What percentage of UK electricity comes from non-renewable sources in total?
    → Nearly 80%.
  36. What conclusion can be made about the majority of UK electricity sources?
    → They are non-renewable.
  37. Why is electricity considered an energy carrier rather than a resource?
    → Because it depends on other sources for production.
  38. What type of energy transfer occurs when radiators warm a room?
    → Thermal energy transfer to the air.
  39. Which renewable heating system uses sunlight directly?
    → Solar water heaters.
  40. What is the main environmental advantage of using renewable energy sources?
    → They reduce dependence on fossil fuels and lower pollution.
