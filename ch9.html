Periodic Table — Short Questions with Answers

  1. Who devised the periodic table?
    → Dmitri Mendeleev.
  2. What nationality was Mendeleev?
    → Russian.
  3. In which century did Mendeleev create the periodic table?
    → The 19th century.
  4. What did the periodic table organize?
    → All known elements.
  5. What did Mendeleev’s table predict?
    → New elements not yet discovered.
  6. What is everything in the universe made from?
    → Different combinations of elements.
  7. How is the periodic table read?
    → From left to right, then top to bottom.
  8. What are elements arranged by in the periodic table?
    → Increasing atomic number.
  9. What does the atomic number represent?
    → The number of protons in the atom.
  10. Where is the atomic number usually found in an element’s box?
    → At the bottom left.
  11. What does the mass number represent?
    → The total number of protons and neutrons.
  12. Where is the mass number usually shown in an element’s box?
    → At the top left.
  13. What does the elemental symbol represent?
    → The one- or two-letter symbol of the element.
  14. What is the elemental symbol for sodium?
    → Na.
  15. What does the atomic number tell us about an element?
    → How many protons its atoms have.
  16. What does the mass number tell us?
    → The total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus.
  17. What repeating feature did Mendeleev notice about the elements?
    → Patterns of chemical properties repeated at regular intervals.
  18. What is a “period” on the periodic table?
    → A horizontal row of elements.
  19. What is a “group” on the periodic table?
    → A vertical column of elements with similar properties.
  20. How are the elements in a group related?
    → They have similar chemical properties.
  21. How many groups are there from the far left to the far right?
    → Seven (plus group 0).
  22. What is the far-right group called?
    → Group 0 (the noble gases).
  23. Why is the far-right group called group 0 instead of group 8?
    → Because their outer shells are full and stable.
  24. Which block of elements between groups 2 and 3 does not have group numbers?
    → The transition metals.
  25. Why do elements in a group have similar chemical properties?
    → They have the same number of outer-shell electrons.
  26. What determines how an element reacts?
    → The number of electrons in its outer shell.
  27. What does the group number tell you about the electrons?
    → How many electrons are in the outer shell.
  28. How many outer electrons do elements in group 2 have?
    → Two.
  29. How many total electrons do all elements in a group have — same or different?
    → Different total numbers of electrons.
  30. What are group 1 elements called?
    → The alkali metals.
  31. How many outer electrons do group 1 elements have?
    → One.
  32. How do group 1 elements react with water?
    → They react violently.
  33. How does reactivity change as you go down group 1?
    → It increases.
  34. What are group 7 elements called?
    → The halogens.
  35. How many outer electrons do group 7 elements have?
    → Seven.
  36. How does reactivity change as you go down group 7?
    → It decreases.
  37. What are group 0 elements called?
    → The noble gases.
  38. Why are noble gases unreactive?
    → They have full outer electron shells.
  39. What does the zigzag line on the periodic table separate?
    → Metals (left) and non-metals (right).
  40. How can you tell which number is the atomic number on a periodic table?
    → It’s the smaller of the two numbers shown.
